Coastal Kenya's Natural Wonders

Where conservation meets adventure in pristine natural settings

Beyond the beaches, Kenya's coast is home to remarkable biodiversity and conservation success stories. From rehabilitated quarries turned nature parks to intimate wildlife sanctuaries, discover how nature and community tourism work together to create unforgettable experiences.

Ngomongo Villages

An eco-cultural village rehabilitated from a former limestone quarry

Experience the remarkable transformation of Ngomongo Villages, where a former limestone quarry has been reborn as a vibrant eco-cultural destination. This unique blend of sustainability, culture, and community tourism showcases how environmental restoration can create meaningful experiences for visitors while supporting local communities.

What Makes Ngomongo Special:

  • Environmental restoration success story
  • Community-driven tourism
  • Sustainable practices
  • Cultural immersion experiences

Butterfly House

A tranquil sanctuary for delicate butterfly observation

Step into a world of delicate beauty at the Butterfly House, where visitors can observe these magnificent creatures up close in their natural habitat. This tranquil sanctuary offers a quiet, intimate experience perfect for nature lovers, photographers, and anyone seeking a peaceful escape into the world of butterflies.

What Makes Butterfly House Special:

  • Intimate butterfly encounters
  • Tranquil and peaceful atmosphere
  • Perfect for nature photography
  • Ideal for quiet contemplation
