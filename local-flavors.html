Coastal Kenya's Culinary Heart

Where traditional Swahili flavors meet fresh local ingredients

Experience the authentic taste of coastal Kenya through bustling markets, street food adventures, and elegant dining experiences. From the vibrant Marikiti Market to the sophisticated Tamarind Dhow Cruise, discover how food connects people, culture, and tradition in this coastal paradise.

Marikiti Market & Forodhani Street Food

Dive into authentic local life with fresh produce, spices, and popular Swahili snacks

Immerse yourself in the vibrant atmosphere of Marikiti Market, where locals gather to buy and sell fresh fruits, aromatic spices, and the freshest seafood. Then, experience the culinary magic of Forodhani Street Food, where you can sample beloved Swahili snacks like mshikaki (grilled meat skewers) and viazi karai (spiced potato fritters).

What You'll Discover:

  • Fresh tropical fruits and vegetables
  • Aromatic spices and seasonings
  • Fresh seafood from local fishermen
  • Authentic Swahili street food

Recommended Partners:

  • Royalty Vacation Homes - Local accommodation and market tour guides

Tamarind Dhow Cruise

A fine-dining dhow experience with panoramic ocean views

Embark on an elegant culinary journey aboard the Tamarind Dhow Cruise, where fine dining meets traditional maritime culture. This sophisticated experience combines panoramic ocean views with exceptional cuisine, creating an unforgettable evening of cultural immersion and gastronomic delight as you sail along Kenya's stunning coastline.

What Makes Tamarind Special:

  • Traditional dhow sailing experience
  • Fine dining with local ingredients
  • Panoramic ocean views
  • Romantic sunset and evening cruises

Immersive Culinary Experiences

Market Tours

Guided tours through local markets with expert food guides who explain ingredients, traditions, and local customs.

Cooking Classes

Learn to prepare traditional Swahili dishes from local chefs using authentic techniques and fresh ingredients.

Seafood Experiences

Visit local fishermen, learn about sustainable fishing practices, and enjoy the freshest catch of the day.

Spice Tours

Discover the aromatic world of coastal spices and learn how they're used in traditional Swahili cuisine.

Must-Try Local Specialties

Mshikaki

Grilled meat skewers marinated in aromatic spices, a beloved street food favorite.

Viazi Karai

Spiced potato fritters, crispy on the outside and soft inside, perfect with chutney.

Pilau

Fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, meat, and vegetables, a Swahili classic.

Coconut Fish

Fresh fish cooked in rich coconut milk with local herbs and spices.

Planning Your Culinary Journey

Best Time to Visit

Early morning for markets, evening for street food, sunset for dhow cruises.

What to Wear

Comfortable clothing and shoes for market exploration. Smart casual for dhow cruises.

Photography

Capture the vibrant colors and energy of markets. Ask permission before photographing people.

Food Safety

Our guides ensure you visit the best, cleanest food vendors and restaurants.
